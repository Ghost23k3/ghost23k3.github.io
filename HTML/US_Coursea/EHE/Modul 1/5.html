Passive Attacks:
  • Do not tamper with the data and intercepting and monitoring network traffic and data flow on the target network
  • Example: Sniffing and eavesdropping (Mithören)
  • It is passive, you are not active in, there is no interaction
  • Sniffing the traffic with a Laptop / Use an antenna to hear a conversation
    • Active Attacks:
  • Tamper with the data in transit or disrupt the communication or service between the systems to bypass or break into secured systems
  • Examples: DoS, Man-in-the-Middle, session hijacking, and SQL injection
  • It is active, there is an interaction
    • Close-in Attacks
  • They are performed when the attacker is in close physical proximity with the target system or network in order to gather, modify, or disrupt access to information.
  • Examples: Social Engineering such as eavesdropping, shoulder sniffing, dumpster diving
  • Attacks over the internet are common, but what is it when you have a cyberattack next door.
  • Dressed like a delivery guy, but is a hacker
    • Insider Attacks
  • When somebody works for the company, violates the rule and attentionally causing damage/destruction to the company
  • Stealing a physical device
  • Involve using privileged access to violate rules or intentionally cause a threat to the organization’s information or information systems
  • Examples: Theft of physical device and planting keyloggers, backdoors, and malware
    • Distirbution Attacks
  • Occur when attackers tamper with hardware of software prior to installation
  • Attackers tamper with the hardware or software at its source or in transit
  • Example: Preinstalling software (Dell, HP), when we have a contract with them, their employees installed a compromised OS, ...
